Topic no. 414, Rental income and expenses
Source: IRS Topic no. 414 page

IRS summary points:
- Cash or the fair market value of property or services received for the use of real estate or personal property is generally taxable rental income.
- Real estate rental income and expenses are generally reported on Schedule E.
- If substantial services are primarily for the tenant's convenience, rental income and expenses are generally reported on Schedule C instead.
- For tax years 2018 through 2025, rental expenses are deductible only if incurred in carrying on a trade or business, for the production of income, or for the management, conservation, or maintenance of property held for the production of income.

Limitations section:
- If you do not use the rental property as a home and rent it for profit, deductible rental expenses can exceed gross rental income, subject to limits.
- For those limits, the IRS specifically directs taxpayers to Publication 925 and Topic no. 425.
