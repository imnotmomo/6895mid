Publication 527 (2025), Residential Rental Property
Source: IRS Publication 527 page

Passive activity limits section:
- In most cases, all rental real estate activities are passive activities unless an exception applies, such as certain real estate professionals.
- A rental activity is generally an activity where income is received mainly for the use of tangible property rather than for services.
- Deductions or losses from passive activities are limited.
- You generally cannot offset nonpassive income with passive losses, and you generally cannot offset tax on nonpassive income with passive credits.
- Excess passive loss or credit is generally carried forward to the next tax year.
- Exceptions for personal use of a dwelling and rental real estate with active participation are discussed in the publication.

Cross-reference useful for RAG:
- Publication 527 explicitly points readers back to Publication 925 for the discussion of rental activities that are not treated as rental activities and for broader passive activity rules.
