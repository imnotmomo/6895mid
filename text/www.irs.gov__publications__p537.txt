Publication 537, Installment Sales.

This document summarizes IRS guidance on installment-sale reporting.

Basic rule:
An installment sale is a disposition of property where at least one payment is received after the tax year of the sale.

Installment method:
Under the installment method, gain is generally reported proportionately as payments are received rather than entirely in the year of sale, unless an exception or election applies.

Gross profit percentage:
The fraction used to determine gain recognized on each principal payment is the gross profit percentage. Gross profit percentage generally equals gross profit divided by contract price.

Gross profit:
Gross profit is generally selling price minus adjusted basis minus selling expenses, with adjustments as described in the publication.

Contract price:
Contract price reflects the amount the seller is entitled to receive for the property under the installment-sale rules and is used in the denominator of the gross profit percentage.

Interest:
Stated or imputed interest is not part of the gain percentage calculation. Interest is generally reported separately from gain.

Depreciation recapture and certain gain components:
Recapture income and certain other amounts may have to be recognized in the year of sale rather than deferred under the installment method.

Related-party and special-property rules:
The publication describes special rules for related-party sales and other special situations.

Cross-reference to asset-disposition rules:
Publication 537 works together with Publication 544 and Publication 551 because installment-sale questions often still require determination of adjusted basis, realized gain, and asset character.

Useful decision sequence for installment-sale questions:
1. Confirm that at least one payment is received after the year of sale.
2. Compute selling price, adjusted basis, selling expenses, and gross profit.
3. Compute contract price.
4. Compute gross profit percentage.
5. Apply that percentage to each principal payment received to determine recognized gain.
6. Separate and report any interest component.
7. Check for recapture or special rules that require immediate recognition.
