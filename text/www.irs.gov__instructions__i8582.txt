Instructions for Form 8582 (2025)
Source: IRS Instructions for Form 8582

Purpose of Form 8582:
- Form 8582 is used by noncorporate taxpayers to figure the amount of any passive activity loss for the current tax year and to report the application of prior-year unallowed passive activity losses.
- A passive activity loss occurs when total passive losses, including prior-year unallowed losses, exceed total passive income.
- Generally, passive activities include trade or business activities in which you did not materially participate and rental activities regardless of participation, unless an exception applies.

Key filing rule:
- Passive activity losses cannot be used to offset nonpassive income unless an exception applies.
- Passive losses not allowed currently are carried forward until allowed against passive income, the special rental real estate allowance, or a fully taxable disposition of the entire interest to an unrelated party.

Exception highlighted by the IRS:
- You may not have to file Form 8582 if your only passive activities are rental real estate activities with active participation, you have no prior-year unallowed losses or credits, your total loss does not exceed $25,000 or $12,500 for certain married filing separately taxpayers, and your modified AGI is within the IRS threshold.

Coordination with other limitations:
- Passive activity losses are subject to other limitations, such as basis and at-risk limits, before passive loss limitations are applied.
- Once a loss is allowable under basis and at-risk rules, you then determine whether it is limited under the passive loss rules.
- The instructions specifically direct taxpayers to Form 6198 for at-risk rules.
- After other loss limitations and passive loss limitations, noncorporate taxpayers may still need Form 461 for the excess business loss limitation.

Trade or business activities section:
- A trade or business activity for these rules is not a rental activity or an activity treated as incidental to holding property for investment.
- These activities are generally reported on Schedule C, Schedule F, or Part II or III of Schedule E.

Special allowance for rental real estate activities:
- If you actively participated in a passive rental real estate activity, you may be able to deduct up to $25,000 of loss from the activity from nonpassive income.
- The special allowance is not available if you were married, filed separately, and lived with your spouse at any time during the year.
- Limited partners are not treated as actively participating in a partnership's rental real estate activity.
