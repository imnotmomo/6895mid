Topic no. 425, Passive activities – Losses and credits
Source: IRS Topic no. 425

IRS summary points:
- Active participation is a less stringent standard than material participation.
- Publication 925 contains the guidelines for determining material participation, real estate professional rules, active participation, and special rules for publicly traded partnership passive items.

Disposition of entire interest:
- Generally, you may fully deduct previously disallowed passive activity losses in the year you dispose of your entire interest in the activity.
- Unused passive activity credits are treated differently. You generally may not claim unused passive activity credits merely because you disposed of your entire interest.
- However, you may elect to increase the basis of credit property by the portion of unused credit that previously reduced basis.

Forms used:
- Use Form 8582, Passive Activity Loss Limitations, to summarize passive income and losses and compute deductible passive losses.
- Use Form 8582-CR, Passive Activity Credit Limitations, to summarize passive credits and compute allowed passive credits.
